About Me

I started my design journey as a college biochemistry student when I designed and presented my first scientific poster, and my passion since then has always been visualization - turning abstract concepts and complex data into beautiful materials that bring the data to life for all users. It’s really driven my work no matter what I’ve been doing; as a part of several research studies, I’ve volunteered to create and modify forms, study materials, and presentations whenever possible. I even designed - and, in five cases, implemented - the poster template used study-wide to present research at conferences like the American Diabetes Association’s Scientific Sessions. And now, I’m looking to fully step into my love of design and make it my career.

CJ and their wife Meg

CJ and the house plants

The other passion in my life has always been accessibility, a passion which has only grown as I became disabled myself. I believe the internet - and our world in general! - is at its best when it’s welcoming to and designed for everyone, and that, in so many cases, exclusion exists simply because of a lack of knowledge and experience. It’s my goal to use my experience - both personal and professional - to narrow that gap and build a more welcoming online experience, one design at a time, one step at a time. After all, very few things in life are inherently exclusionary or inaccessible, and even fewer truly can't be improved or even fixed if we just remember to center our most vulnerable users and their experiences.
